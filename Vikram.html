Vikram Sarabhai

Father of India's Space Program

Early Life

Vikram Sarabhai was born on 12 August 1919 in Ahmedabad, India. His father, Ambalal Sarabhai, was a famous industrialist and his mother, Sarla Devi, was a devoted social worker. Vikram was the third of eight children in the family.

Career

Vikram Sarabhai was a brilliant scientist and innovator. He studied at Cambridge University and earned a PhD in physics in 1947. He returned to India and founded the Physical Research Laboratory (PRL) in Ahmedabad in 1948. PRL is now a leading research institute in the field of space sciences in India.

Sarabhai played a key role in the establishment of the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR) in 1962, which later became the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He served as the chairman of INCOSPAR from 1963 to 1972 and as the chairman of ISRO from 1972 to 1975. He was instrumental in the development of India's first satellite, Aryabhata, and the successful launch of India's first satellite, Rohini, in 1980.

Awards and Honours

  • Padma Bhushan (1966)
  • Padma Vibhushan (1972)
  • International Astronautical Federation's Gold Medal (1972)
  • United Nations Peace Medal (1975)
